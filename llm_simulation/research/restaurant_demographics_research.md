# Restaurant Consumer Behaviour — Demographics Research
*Compiled from National Restaurant Association, OpenTable, Toast POS, McKinsey, Deloitte, Statista, YouGov, PYMNTS (2024–2026)*

---

## By Age Group

### Gen Z (18–25)
- **Dining frequency:** Most frequent diners; 71% plan to increase dining frequency
- **Spend per visit:** Higher than other generations per visit; share dishes to manage cost
- **Alcohol:** 25% order alcoholic beverages (vs. 50% Millennials)
- **Discovery:** 77% find restaurants through social media; 72% trust social media reviews
- **Research behaviour:** Google/Yelp for research; TikTok/Instagram for discovery
- **Solo dining:** 68% have dined solo at sit-down restaurants; 65% plan to continue
- **Tech adoption:** 82% use digital wallets; 67% use mobile ordering; 69% would use voice ordering
- **Cuisine diversity:** Prefer international options in culturally authentic settings; flexitarian-leaning
- **Delivery:** 65% engage in takeout/delivery
- **Service preference:** 58% receptive to automation/robotic service (vs. 42% for 39+)
- **Print vs QR:** 90% prefer print menus despite digital fluency
- **Loyalty type:** Social-media discovery-driven; explore frequently; price-aware

### Young Millennials (26–33)
- **Dining frequency:** 14×/month (highest of any generation, 2026)
- **Plans:** 68% plan to increase dining frequency
- **Mobile ordering:** 74% use mobile ordering
- **Alcohol:** 50% name alcoholic drinks as regular beverage
- **Discovery:** 79% consider Instagram/TikTok-worthiness in selection
- **Social dining:** 77% say dining out serves social plans
- **Solo dining:** 63% have dined solo; likely to continue
- **Research:** Google and Yelp primary; social media secondary
- **Cuisine diversity:** High; open to experimentation; premium casual preference
- **Loyalty type:** Balanced; social occasion driver; builds favourites but explores regularly

### Senior Millennials (34–40)
- Same dining frequency range as Young Millennials (12–14×/month)
- Higher disposable income; preference for full-service over fast casual
- Family dining occasions becoming more frequent (children present)
- Quality consistency more important than novelty at this stage

### Gen X (41–56)
- **Weekly dining (high income households):** 64% of $100k+ dine weekly
- **Table service preference:** 71% prefer traditional table service
- **Digital wallets:** 75% comfortable with digital payments
- **Chef's table/tasting:** ~50% likely to participate
- **Research:** Online reviews; less social-media driven
- **Price sensitivity:** Moderate; value quality-price ratio
- **Loyalty type:** Moderate loyalty; fewer but reliable restaurant choices

### Boomers (57+)
- **Weekly dining (low income):** 42% of <$50k households dine weekly
- **Time spent eating:** Ages 65–74 average 81.6 min/day; 75+ average 88.2 min/day
- **Chef's table:** Only one-third likely to participate
- **Digital wallets:** Only one-third comfortable
- **Voice ordering:** 26% would use
- **Service preference:** 80% prefer traditional service; 90% prefer print menus
- **Yelp representation:** 39% of Yelp users are 55+
- **Price sensitivity:** Moderate; value over novelty
- **Loyalty type:** Very high; same restaurants for years

## By Occupation / Income

### High-Income ($200k+) / Executives
- **Share of industry spend:** 24% of total food-away-from-home spending
- **Weekly dining rate:** 64%
- **Venue preference:** Full-service restaurants; experiential dining
- **Spend per person:** Significantly above average
- **Cuisine diversity:** High; willing to try emerging cuisines
- **Loyalty:** Moderate; value quality and exclusivity

### Upper-Middle Income ($100k–$199k) / Tech / Finance / Legal
- **Share of industry spend:** 35%
- **Weekly dining rate:** 55%
- **Mix:** Full-service and casual dining
- **Price sensitivity:** Low–moderate
- **Loyalty type:** Regular haunts + occasional exploration

### Office / Corporate Workers
- **Weekday buying:** 29% make restaurant purchases on weekdays
- **Lunch patterns:** Traditional noon lunch; shifting with hybrid work
- **Quick-service:** Two-thirds likely to buy from QSR
- **Hybrid workers:** Eat out 3×/week average
- **In-office workers:** Traditional weekday lunch market

### Remote Workers
- **Weekend dining:** 36% visit restaurants primarily on weekends
- **Lunch:** 75% get lunch from restaurant ≥1×/week; 31% dine out for lunch 3×/week
- **Quick-service:** 56% likely (vs. 67% for in-office)
- **At-home lunch:** 49% make at home; 42% use delivery; 41% eat leftovers
- **Impact:** Shifted lunch volumes; demand spreads across day

### Healthcare / Shift Workers
- **Eating habits:** 2× higher odds of poor eating habits vs. day workers
- **Pattern:** Irregular eating; frequent meal skipping
- **Food sourcing:** Majority from canteens/cafeterias during shifts
- **External dining:** Limited during shifts; access constrained by timing
- **Social driver:** Colleagues biggest influence when dining out
- **Decision factors:** Accessibility and timing > preference for food

### Students / Academic
- **Budget:** Primary constraint; shared plates, deals, happy hours
- **Frequency:** 2–3×/week; lower spend per visit
- **Discovery:** Social media + peer recommendations
- **Cuisine:** Open to international; street food; authentic over branded
- **Solo dining:** Comfortable; often study-adjacent café-restaurants
- **Delivery:** Heavy use for convenience

### Trade / Manual Workers
- **Timing:** Early lunch (11 AM–12:30 PM); early dinner (4:30–6 PM)
- **Venue type:** Diners, fast casual, pubs; value-driven
- **Price sensitivity:** High; value-first
- **Cuisine:** Familiar, hearty; not adventurous
- **Group dining:** Often with colleagues on breaks
- **Delivery:** Low usage

### Creative / Media / Freelance
- **Frequency:** 3–4×/week; café-restaurants blur with coffee visits
- **Discovery:** Heavy Instagram/TikTok; want shareworthy venues
- **Cuisine:** High diversity appetite; ethnic, trendy, experiential
- **Group size:** Small groups (2–4); occasional solo
- **Price sensitivity:** Moderate; experience-over-value trade-off
- **Loyalty type:** Neighbourhood loyal; champions local independents

## Key Behavioural Dimensions

### Dining Frequency (Monthly Average, 2026 Baseline: 10×)
| Group | Visits/Month |
|-------|-------------|
| Millennials 26–40 | 14 |
| Gen Z 18–25 | 10–12 |
| High-income households | 10+ |
| Gen X 41–56 | 8–10 |
| Boomers 57+ | 6–8 |
| Remote workers (weekday lunch) | 8–12 |
| Healthcare shift workers | 4–6 (limited) |

### Average Spend Per Visit (2024)
- Monthly average all consumers: $191 (up from $166 in 2023)
- Delivery/takeout monthly: $88.50
- Solo diners: 48% more per person; 31% have higher bills
- QSR price points: Sandwiches $11.26; Bowls $12.98; Burgers $11.45

### Cuisine Diversity & Loyalty
- 65% of delivery/takeout customers prefer familiar cuisines
- Gen Z most adventurous; Boomers most conservative
- Decision hierarchy: Food quality → ratings → price → service → atmosphere

### Delivery vs. Dine-In (2026)
- 55% prefer dine-in (up from 43% in 2023)
- Takeout preferred over delivery: 65% vs. 35%
- Fast food dominates takeout (63%), fast casual (47%), casual dining (34%)

### Group Size
- 81% of Americans have dined solo at least once
- 52% plan solo dining in 2026
- Group dining up 11% YoY
- 40% prefer group eating vs. solo
