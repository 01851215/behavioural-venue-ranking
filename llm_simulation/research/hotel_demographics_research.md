# Hotel & Accommodation Consumer Behaviour — Demographics Research
*Compiled from J.D. Power, GBTA, Hilton Trends Report, Skift, Expedia, STR/CoStar, Emerald Insight (2024–2025)*

---

## By Age Group

### Gen Z (18–25)
- **Business vs leisure:** Primarily leisure; emerging business (entry-level professionals)
- **Accommodation type:** ~60% prefer hotels; 54% willing to pay premium for sustainability
- **Brand loyalty:** LOW — 39% in loyalty programs (vs. 66% Boomers); don't chase points
- **Boutique vs chain:** Boutique, unique, Instagram-worthy; avoid generic branded properties
- **Top amenities:**
  1. Free WiFi (26% cite as #1)
  2. Digital/mobile experience (83% want fully online booking; 84% want seamless device integration)
  3. Authentic, locally inspired design
  4. Sustainability features
- **Review reliance:** Heavy reliance on social media, peer recommendations, TikTok
- **Booking lead time:** Spontaneous; often <7 days; mobile-first
- **Price sensitivity:** Moderate; experience-over-price but budget-conscious
- **Key insight:** Responsible for 80% of luxury travel spending in certain sub-segments (high-earning Gen Z)

### Young Millennials (26–33)
- **Business vs leisure:** Bleisure (both); 42% will spend MORE on travel in 2025
- **Accommodation type:** ~60% prefer hotels; also favour B&Bs and inns
- **Brand loyalty:** Growing — 45.6% brand loyal; 51% in loyalty programs
- **Boutique vs chain:** Boutique-leaning; desire unique non-branded feel while earning points
- **Top amenities:**
  1. Free cancellation (70% prioritise)
  2. Meals included (62%)
  3. Free hotel upgrades (58%)
  4. WiFi + breakfast (expected as standard)
  5. Culinary experiences, local dining
- **Review reliance:** Very high — 82% consider reviews important; read multiple platforms
- **Booking lead time:** Leisure: 20–60 days; Business: 7–10 days
- **Price sensitivity:** Moderate; seek flexibility features over pure discounts; 67% pay premium for "vibe"
- **Bleisure behaviour:** 49% use business travel loyalty points for leisure

### Senior Millennials (34–40)
- Similar to Young Millennials but higher income; family travel occasions increasing
- Quality and family amenities (kids' facilities, pools, breakfast) grow in priority
- Higher ADR tolerance; reliability and space over novelty

### Gen X (41–56)
- **Business vs leisure:** High business travel; balanced with leisure
- **Accommodation type:** Hotel dominant; 56% in loyalty programs
- **Brand loyalty:** HIGHEST of any generation — pays premium for familiar brands
- **Boutique vs chain:** Strong chain preference; value consistency and predictability
- **Top amenities:**
  1. High-speed internet (77.3% consider critical)
  2. Room cleanliness and comfort
  3. Location convenience to workplace/venue
  4. Free breakfast (now expected)
- **Review reliance:** Best online researchers — carefully read reviews; 83% research online; only 17% use social media
- **Booking lead time:** 30–60 days for leisure; business 7–10 days
- **Price sensitivity:** Low — quality assurance > price; not deal-driven

### Boomers (57+) / Retirees
- **Business vs leisure:** Almost exclusively leisure (65+ retired)
- **Accommodation type:** 73.4% strongly prefer hotels; avoid short-term rentals
- **Brand loyalty:** Extremely high — 66% in loyalty programs; "ideal chain hotel customer"
- **Boutique vs chain:** Chain-preferred; gravitating toward lifestyle brands (Hotel Indigo, Andaz)
- **Top amenities:**
  1. Culinary experiences (45% top travel budget priority; 86% seek local cuisine)
  2. Wellness/spa
  3. Accessibility features (grab bars, non-slip flooring, ergonomic furniture)
  4. Sleep quality emphasis
- **Review reliance:** Moderate — 66% influenced by online ads; only 10% use social media
- **Booking lead time:** Extended — multiple months; travel in non-peak periods
- **Price sensitivity:** Low — disposable income from pension/retirement; comfort > cost
- **Key insight:** Responsible for 80% of luxury travel spending; longest average stay (1–3 weeks)

## By Occupation

### Business Travelers / Corporate Professionals
- **Business vs leisure:** 100% business primary; 49% save loyalty for leisure
- **Brand loyalty:** HIGH — 65% in loyalty programs; 82% say programs matter in booking; 81% believe members get better service; 90% view rewards as motivating
- **Boutique vs chain:** Chain-preferred; reliability and reward integration
- **Top amenities:**
  1. High-speed WiFi (critical for productivity)
  2. Convenient location (proximity to meeting venue)
  3. Best price/value (within corporate policy)
  4. Express check-in/out
- **Loyalty rewards priority:** Free nights > room upgrades > flexibility > express check-in > service perks
- **Review reliance:** Moderate; location/logistics trump review scores
- **Booking lead time:** SHORT — 7–10 days; 50% within 10 days; 80% within 21 days
- **Price sensitivity:** LOW — average $180/night vs. $145 leisure; ROI-focused
- **Key insight:** 71% willing to book out of corporate policy to earn loyalty points

### Healthcare Workers (Travel Nurses / Allied Health)
- **Type:** 100% business/assignment-based; temporary relocations (3–12 months)
- **Brand loyalty:** Low–moderate; driven by stipend and practical needs
- **Boutique vs chain:** Extended-stay hotels or furnished apartments strongly preferred
- **Top amenities:**
  1. Full kitchen (home-like layout)
  2. Flexible lease terms (short-term commitment)
  3. Location near hospital/clinic
  4. Affordable (maximise housing stipend)
  5. Practical amenities for shift consistency
- **Review reliance:** Peer communities (travel nurse networks); practical community-based
- **Booking lead time:** Assignment-dependent (weeks to months via agency)
- **Price sensitivity:** HIGH — maximising tax-free housing stipend; cost-per-amenity analysis
- **Key insight:** Often share housing with other travel nurses; wellness amenities important

### Academic / Research Professionals
- **Type:** 100% business (conferences, collaborations, sabbaticals)
- **Brand loyalty:** Moderate; driven by institutional preferences and conference hotel blocks
- **Boutique vs chain:** No strong preference; proximity to venue and cost are primary
- **Top amenities:**
  1. High-speed WiFi (critical for research work and presentations)
  2. Quiet work environment + desk space
  3. Location near university / conference centre
  4. 24-hour access
  5. Library/reference access nearby
- **Review reliance:** Moderate–high; peer recommendations; sustainability valued
- **Booking lead time:** Conference-dependent — often 2–6 months (institutional procurement)
- **Price sensitivity:** MODERATE–HIGH — budget-conscious; institutional rate programmes
- **Key insight:** Often alone or small groups; extended stays (3–30 days)

### Remote Workers / Digital Nomads
- **Type:** Hybrid — 50% work / 50% leisure; "work from anywhere"
- **Brand loyalty:** VERY LOW — constantly exploring new destinations
- **Boutique vs chain:** Diverse; boutique, co-living, long-stay hotels; avoid generic chains
- **Top amenities:**
  1. High-speed internet (50+ Mbps symmetric; 100+ Mbps benchmark) — CRITICAL
  2. Dedicated workspace/ergonomic desk (41% prioritise)
  3. Co-working space access/proximity
  4. Social/community spaces (combat isolation)
  5. Reliable power outlets
- **Booking patterns:** 66% prefer 3–6 month stays; 80% prefer 3–9 months per location; monthly rates
- **Review reliance:** High; nomad-specific platforms (Nomad List, co-living community reviews)
- **Price sensitivity:** Moderate; pay for reliable internet; monthly rate discount expected
- **Key insight:** 69% seeking co-working spaces; prefer cafés and co-working over hotel lobby for work

### Leisure / Vacation Travelers (General)
- **Type:** 100% leisure; increasing bleisure extension
- **Brand loyalty:** Moderate–low (less loyal than business travellers)
- **Top amenities:**
  1. Free breakfast (63% prioritise; 24% cite as #1)
  2. WiFi (26% #1 priority; now expected)
  3. Fitness/gym (wellness trend)
  4. Sustainability features (60% say crucial; 67% pay premium for vibe)
  5. Pool/sports facilities
- **Review reliance:** VERY HIGH — 82% consider reviews important; 79% more likely to choose higher-rated; average 9 reviews read before booking
- **Review platforms:** Google Reviews (now most influential), TripAdvisor, Booking.com
- **Booking lead time:** 30–60 days (international 60+ days)
- **Price sensitivity:** HIGHER than business; comparison-shop more; value-conscious

2. Free breakfast (63% prioritise)
3. Cleanliness + comfort (baseline expectation)
4. Convenient location
